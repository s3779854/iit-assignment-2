AUGMENTED REALITY

What does it do?

Augmented reality (AR) is technology that uses sounds, images and test to then interact and effect the world we see. It creates videos and pictures which adds a layer to reality to create experience of characters and images being there. Augmented reality does focus specifically on the use of mobile technology such as cameras in smart phones and GPS location. However, it is inexpensive for users of smartphones compared to Virtual Reality (VR) which is like AR technology, but instead is completely altering the reality. 
Augmented reality is currently being used for a variety of novelty effects such as Snapchat and Instagram filters, but also is used for various beneficial applications such as navigation systems, furniture companies and even sport broadcasters.

AR is currently being used in a variety of smartphone apps such as Pokémon Go. Pokémon GO use AR technology to create the feel as if Pokémon are there with the camera showing the real world with a Pokémon in front of it. It influenced the amount of AR games in the smartphone industry as many games tried to follow suit and have the same sort of feel to their game.
The earliest record of Augmented Reality to have been created and used is in 1968, a professor, Ivan Sutherland, invented the first augmented reality device called The Sword of Damocles. With the help of his student, Bob Sproull, The Sword of Damocles was a head-mounted display that hung from the ceiling and the user would feel as if they were in an alternate reality because of computerized graphics. Even though, this is closer to Virtual Reality, it created a massive steppingstone for the development of AR.
In 1992, a research lab, USAF, created the first mainstream AR system that was the earliest version of Augmented Reality available to the public. The AR was called Virtual Fixtures and essentially placed information in front of the users work environment, to make it interactive. This system was the model unit to then create the AR we know in the modern day.

The development for augmented reality is rapidly increasing as the need for AR is increasing. AR is in higher demand for medical purposes, research and military purposes. With smart devices and medical machines improving over time, augmented reality will slowly follow suit with the growth and development. It has also impacted the travel industry as it has grown in popularity. Travel companies have reported that hotels and popular tourist attractions have adopted AR technology to encourage tourists to visit or stay at their local hotels. AR gives customers the opportunity to have information on popular tourist locations 24/7.

The future for AR isn't limited to novelty items and applications on smartphones. Augmented reality is being researched into being used for contact lenses and glasses. Google did attempt this with the Google Glass in 2013 but was unsuccessful. However, AR research is being slowly introduced into the medical world by helping individuals' issues with sight or who are completely blind. It is also helping nurses and doctors currently help find veins easier within a patient with a hand-held scanner that has AR technology to make veins easier to find.
Social media applications will also see an increase of AR use as many platforms have adopted filters. AR has a close connection with Artificial Intelligence (AI) so the development of AI and AR will also sufficiently increase. According to research, the total number of active social media users currently sits at 3.8 billion. Social media apps like Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat all have AR and AI technology embedded in their application. With filters on the rise and with Snapchat and Instagram having over 1million active users currently, it is unsurprising how in demand it is.

What does it do?

Data Science and Data Analytics are two different topics but related in the same area. Data Science is cleaning and preparing data through methods of mathematics, programming and use of statistics. Whereas Data Analytics is looking through the data and finding the correlation between the sets of data, this may involve mechanical processing or algorithmic processing. Data Science and Data Analytics come hand in hand when it comes to job professions and topic of research.
Data Analytics partakes in a huge role within the marketing and financial industry as Data Analytics is used to predict trends in markets, the potential risks and helps to prevent fraud. Without Data Analytics, finances would have no efficient way on adjusting necessary prices, fraud that could ruin a company and lose thousands of money and stock from market trends going undetected. 

It is currently also used within research and medical facilities, crime prevention and environmental issues. Essentially, anything that may have a pattern or trend uses Data Analytics and Data Science.
Currently, healthcare has progressed the most out of all industries to incorporate Data Analytics. It is currently used widespread in machines to help drug discovery, the market for the said drugs and treatment within the hospitals. Without the use of Data Analytics, treatment of patients and the quality of treatment wouldn't be where it is today, millions of people would be dying more of illnesses and viruses compared to the amount today.
Another area that is currently involved with Data Analytics is the travel industry. Travel industry gathers information on customer's preferences and desires, which then leads to personalized travel recommendations. The information also helps indicate if sales are working in relation to the customized offers and customer's needs.

Data Science is commonly used for Internet Search, Recommender Systems and Digital Advertisements. Internet search is how Google and Bing find their best result searches within fraction of seconds through algorithms and specific mathematics. This is to make sure search queries have the best results for users to be able to find what they want.
Recommender Systems follow similar aspects to Internet Search. Essentially, recommender systems find relevant products from products available and creates suggestions based on user's relevance and demands. It sorts through billions of different products and possibly different websites at the same time.

Digital Advertisements are specific digital banners and billboards using data science algorithms based on clickthrough rate (CTR). This gives also gives specific advertisements to users which then leads to their specific products dedicated to their needs.
Both Data Science and Analytics need knowledge of SQL and Python to produce and store the results from all the data. However, the outcome of the two will be different. One, data science, will focus on the problems and patterns whereas Data Analytics design the techniques and solutions to apply.

As machines learn new algorithms and data inputs, it causes the quality of algorithms and tools to increase. CLOUD COMPUTING

What does it do?

Cloud Computing is an on-demand computing service. It’s including servers, storage, databases, networking, software analytics, and intelligence over the internet. Users can access applications and files by logging in from any devices that have an internet connection.

Cloud computing has a few types of cloud services. Use cloud computing can be done with Infrastructure as a service (IaaS), Platform as a service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS). The Infrastructure as service (IaaS) refers to fundamental building blocks of computing. It can rent physical or virtual servers, storage, and networking. This is the most open-ended type of cloud service for organizations that want to do a lot of customization by themselves. And Platform as a service (PaaS) refers to cloud computing service that on-demand environment for developing, testing, delivering, and managing software applications.

Software as a Service (SaaS) is the most common type of cloud computing. It delivers complete, user-ready applications over the internet. These typically do not have to be downloaded and installed on each individual user's computer, saving technical staff lots of time. The maintenance and troubleshooting are handled entirely by the vendor.

Private cloud refers to cloud computing resources used exclusively by a single business or organization. Hybrid cloud is a mix of public and private clouds, it bound together by technology that allows data and application to be shared between them.

This entails securing a budget, setting up the environment through physical assets, significant manpower, and time. Next one is Big data analytics. It have ability to tap into vast quantities of both structured and unstructured data to harness the benefit of extracting business value.

The File storage also can done in cloud computing. Cloud can offer the possibility of storing files and accessing, storing, and retrieving them from any web-enabled interface. This is usually simple, anytime can place it with high availability, speed, scalability, and security for your environment. Disaster recovery also. This provides faster recovery from a mesh of different physical locations at a lower cost. Lastly, the Backup. It has always been a complex and time-consuming operation. This enables enterprises or individuals to store their data and computer file on the Internet using a storage service provider.

Cloud computing is massively on the rise in the current day and age. In fact, 81 percent of companies with 1000 employees or more have a multi-platform strategy. The number will be rise to more than 90 percent by 2024. Between 2018 and 2021, worldwide spending on public cloud services is to grow to 73 percent, which is from $160B to $277B. But Bart McDonough, who is CEO of Agio, believes the recent rapid adoption of cloud is mainly due to understanding of "ease of use and scalability" of the technology. Recently organizations expand their understanding of the enormous benefits of cloud computing, so they are now more willing to conduct workload tests n cloud and even change applications to the cloud.

To make this possible, organizations need to understand more about cloud computing. Something like advantages and disadvantages, to expand their understanding makes them have advantages too, which companies can move faster on a project and test out the concept without so much cost because firms only pay for the resources they consume. The ability to sign up new services without the time and effort associated with traditional IT procurement should mean that is easier to get going with new application faster. Expect more wearable technology and internet-connected devices - from fitness trackers, thermostats, and security systems to refrigerators, pet dishes, and washing machines. CYBERSECURITY

What does it do?

Cybersecurity is the art of protecting systems, networks, devices, and data from unauthorized access or criminal use and the practice to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, which means to protect from cyber-threats. A successful cybersecurity approach has multiple layers of protection spread across the computer networks, programs, or data that one intends to keep safe. To protect the data it's necessary to understand which threats are there.

It has some elements of cybersecurity, ensuring cybersecurity requires the coordination of security efforts made throughout an information system. Firstly, network security is the practice of securing a computer network from intruders. It can protect internal networks from intruders by securing infrastructure. The example is two-factor authentication and a strong new password. The application security is focused on keeping software and devices free of threats. A compromised application could provide access to the data it's designed to protect. This begins in the design stage, well before a program or device is deployed. Information security protects both physical and digital data. Operational security included the processes and decisions for handling and protecting data assets. The permissions users have when accessing a network and the procedures that determine how and where data may be stored or shared. And disaster recovery and business continuity define how an organization responds to a cyber-security incident or any other event that causes the loss of operations or data. Disaster recovery policies dictate how the organization restores operations and information to return to the same operating capacity as before the event. And also users are need to be educated.
